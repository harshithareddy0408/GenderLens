Our Methodology

1. Goal

  • Identify and analyze gender bias in educational materials using NLP techniques.
  • Leverage AI to provide an objective and data-driven approach.
  • Understand how gender representation impacts learning.

2. Why?

  • Gender bias in textbooks influences perceptions from an early age.
  • Reinforces stereotypes affecting career choices, leadership roles, and social dynamics.
  • Our methodology highlights and mitigates these biases to promote equality in education.

3. What?

  • Sentence-Level Analysis: The system breaks down text into individual sentences and filters for those containing gender-related terms using a predefined keyword set.
  • Multi-Feature Classification: Gender-related sentences are processed through three parallel methods: RoBERTa model classification, contextual embedding analysis, and named entity recognition to capture different aspects of potential bias.
  • Bias Threshold Detection: Sentences receive a bias score (0-1) from the RoBERTa model, with scores above 0.5 flagged as potentially biased for further analysis.
  • Counterfactual Testing: For flagged sentences, the system performs gender term swapping (he→she, man→woman) and measures the change in bias score to validate if the bias is genuinely gender-related.
  • Comprehensive Evaluation: The final analysis combines direct classification results with supporting evidence from similarity analysis and entity-attribute patterns to provide a more reliable bias assessment.

4. The "IT" Factor

  • Triple-layered feature extraction: By simultaneously processing text through RoBERTa classification, contextual embeddings, and named entity recognition, the system captures both explicit and implicit gender bias indicators.
  • Counterfactual testing methodology: The gender term swap and re-scoring mechanism provides empirical validation of bias detection, significantly reducing false positives compared to single-pass classification systems.
  • Contextual understanding: The system doesn't simply flag gender-related terms but evaluates their usage in context, distinguishing between descriptive mentions and prescriptive stereotypes.
  • Two-phase filtering approach: The system first identifies gender-related content through comprehensive keyword matching, then applies counterfactual testing only to those sentences - creating a computationally efficient pipeline that maintains high accuracy while reducing processing overhead.
